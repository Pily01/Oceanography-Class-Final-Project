Ocean Circulation

Ocean circulation transports nutrients and organisms along the globe. It influences the weather and climate of the wolrd.

There are two key concepts we need to understand first: winds an currents. Winds are named from a direction in which they are coming, while currents are named for the direction in which they flow.

Wind driven currents move water horixzontally, down to one km. Density driven currents move water vertically. These currents mix different water masses.

Gyres and Eddies

There are five subtropical gyres around the globe: North Atlantic gyre, South Atlantic gyre, Indian Ocean gyre, North Pacific gyre, and South Pacific gyre.

Contrary to gyres, eddies are a circular movement of water formed along the edge of a permanent current. In an average year, 10-15 rings are formed. Most of them are 150-300 km in diameter, and their speed is 1 m/s.

They have a warm core ring that rotates clockwise and a cold core ring that rotates counterclockwise.

Ekman Spiral and Transport

Ekman Spiral

It describes the speed and direction of flow of surface waters at various depths. Factors: wind and Coriolis effect.

Ekman Transport

It is the overall water movement due to the Ekman spiral. The ideal transport is 90 degrees from the wind. Its direction depends on the hemisphere it is in.
